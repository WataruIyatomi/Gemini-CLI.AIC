About Me

A Japanese-American chess prodigy, Hikaru Nakamura is a five-time U.S. Chess Champion and the 2022 Fischer Random World Champion. Beyond his dominance in classical chess, he is widely regarded as one of the world's best blitz and bullet players. He has also become a massive figure in online chess, popularizing the game through his engaging Twitch streams.

Playing Style

Hikaru Nakamura is renowned for his aggressive, dynamic, and often unpredictable playing style. He excels in rapid, blitz, and bullet formats due to his exceptional speed and tactical prowess. Known as "The H Bomb," he combines creative openings with a pragmatic approach, adapting his strategy to dominate opponents.

Early Life & Prodigy Years

Born in Hirakata, Japan, Hikaru Nakamura moved to the U.S. at age two. He began playing chess at seven, coached by his stepfather, FIDE Master Sunil Weeramantry. A true prodigy, he became the youngest American to defeat an International Master at age 10 and earned the chess master title. In 2003, at just 15 years and 79 days old, he became the youngest American Grandmaster at the time, breaking Bobby Fischer's record.
